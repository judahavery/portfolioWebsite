About Me

Growing up as a kid, I played a wide variety of games, but usually only with friends. Movies and tv shows didn't appeal to me because I didn't feel invested. With games, I didn't just experience stories and fantasies, I lived them. Interacting with other people through games hooked me for that reason.

While friends were offline, I turned to a different source of entertainment. I loved to create. Any game with some level creation tool I spent countless hours in, perfecting my "masterpieces." Whether these levels were made in games like Little Big Planet 2 or Halo Reach, I was ecstatic to watch my friends try them out. In games like Call of Duty, rather than honing my skills on the battlefield, I spent my time designing personal emblems and weapon skins in the editor. Even though I couldn't brag about my gameplay skills, I could show off my "flawless" designs. Which felt much more rewarding.

In high school, I found my passion for game design. I had just switched to a tech school where I studied game development and other creative media platforms. I booted up Unreal Engine 4 for the first time and became obsessed. I found the ultimate level editor, a game engine. After that, I poured all of my efforts into learning game development.

I am currently attending Champlain College and expected to graduate in 2023 with a Bachelor of Science in Game Design. During my high school experience, I worked across the full spectrum of game development. I 3D modeled, rigged, animated, and programmed on the technical side along with designing the narrative, levels, and systems for my game projects. At Champlain College, I'm continuing to develop those skills, but I'm also working in larger teams on development projects learning more in depth how to manage workflow within a professional environment.

Crafting unique worlds and experiences for others to enjoy excites me beyond explanation, and someday I hope to influence others with games the way I was impacted by them growing up.
